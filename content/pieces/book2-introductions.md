# Well-Tempered Clavier Book II (BWV 870-893) - Piece Introductions

## Overview

This document contains introductions for all 48 pieces (24 preludes and 24 fugues) from Johann Sebastian Bach's Well-Tempered Clavier Book II, compiled circa 1742.

---

## BWV 870 - C major

### Prelude

**Opening Hook:** The second volume of the Well-Tempered Clavier opens with a prelude of crystalline elegance, establishing a tone of refined maturity that distinguishes Book II from its predecessor.

**Character:** This prelude flows with the gentle inevitability of a clear stream, its character marked by serene confidence and graceful forward motion. The mood is contemplative yet luminous, suggesting the measured pace of thoughtful discourse rather than dramatic declaration. Bach presents C major not as triumphant brilliance but as quiet, assured beauty.

**Notable Features:**
- Two-voice texture throughout creates transparency and demands equal attention to both hands
- Continuous sixteenth-note figuration in perpetual motion style
- Subtle harmonic richness beneath the simple surface texture
- Delayed cadential resolution creates gentle forward momentum

**Listening Focus:** Notice how the two voices interweave in constant dialogue, with the sixteenth-note patterns passing between hands. Listen for the moments where Bach delays expected resolutions, creating gentle suspense within the flowing texture.

**Technical Overview:** Binary form with continuous sixteenth-note motion. Requires exceptional evenness of touch and voicing clarity between two equally important lines. The technical challenge lies in maintaining singing legato while keeping rhythmic precision.

**Historical Context:** Composed approximately two decades after Book I, this prelude reflects Bach's mature Leipzig style, showing greater refinement and subtlety in its treatment of the simplest key.

**Difficulty:** 3/5 | **Study Time:** 8 hours

---

### Fugue (3 voices)

**Opening Hook:** This three-voice fugue balances academic rigor with expressive warmth, its subject combining stepwise motion with gentle leaps in a character both learned and lyrical.

**Character:** The fugue unfolds with quiet dignity, its affect suggesting scholarly contemplation rather than virtuosic display. The mood remains consistently serene, with Bach exploring contrapuntal possibilities through subtle variation rather than dramatic contrast. There is a sense of wise maturity throughout.

**Notable Features:**
- Subject features characteristic descending scale followed by ascending leap
- Frequent use of invertible counterpoint at the octave and tenth
- Stretto entries in the final section create climactic density
- Episode material derives organically from the subject's melodic cells

**Listening Focus:** Follow the subject as it appears in each voice, noting how Bach varies its accompaniment while maintaining recognizability. Pay attention to the increasing density in the final section as voices overlap in stretto.

**Technical Overview:** Three-voice fugue in moderate tempo requiring clarity of voice leading and sensitive dynamic shading. The primary challenge is maintaining three distinct melodic lines with appropriate weight for each entry.

**Historical Context:** Bach's mature fugal writing shows here in the organic integration of episodes with subject material, reflecting his complete mastery of the form developed over decades of practice.

**Difficulty:** 3/5 | **Study Time:** 10 hours
